Japanese Soufflé Pancakes

Servings: 2 Prep: 20 min Cook: 15 min

Light, airy pancakes made by folding whipped meringue into a smooth egg yolk batter.

Ingredients

  • 2 large eggs (50 g each w/o shell)
  • 1½ Tbsp whole milk
  • ¼ tsp pure vanilla extract
  • ¼ cup cake flour
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • 2 Tbsp sugar
  • 1 Tbsp neutral oil (for greasing the pan)
  • 2 Tbsp water (for steaming)

Tools

  • Whisk or hand mixer
  • Mixing bowls & spatula
  • Nonstick pan with lid
  • Ring molds (optional)

Directions

  1. Make yolk batter — Whisk yolks, milk, and vanilla until frothy. Sift in cake flour and baking powder; mix until smooth.
  2. Whip meringue — In a clean bowl, beat egg whites until foamy. Gradually add sugar; whip until glossy medium peaks form.
  3. Fold gently — Add one-third of meringue to yolk mixture, whisk lightly, then fold in the rest in two parts.
  4. Heat & portion — Grease a nonstick pan with oil and preheat over low. Scoop batter into tall mounds; add more batter after 1 minute.
  5. Steam-cook — Add 1 Tbsp water to the pan (not on batter), cover, and cook 6–7 minutes.
  6. Flip & finish — Flip gently, add remaining water, cover, cook 4–5 minutes until jiggly and golden. Serve immediately.
Tip: Keep the heat low! Steam traps moisture and makes pancakes fluffy. Over-mixing the batter will deflate them.

Troubleshooting

  • Flat: Meringue under-whipped or over-folded.
  • Wet center: Not enough cooking or steam.
  • Burning: Heat too high; cook slower under a lid.
