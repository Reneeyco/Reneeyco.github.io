Pacific Rhododendron

Rhododendron macrophyllum — Washington State Flower

Overview

The Pacific rhododendron is a broadleaf evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest. It grows along forest edges, clearings, and mountain slopes in acidic, well-drained soils with cool, moist conditions.

Quick Facts

Scientific name
Rhododendron macrophyllum
Common names
Pacific Rhododendron, Coast Rhododendron
Plant size
Typically 5–15 ft (1.5–4.5 m); occasionally to ~25 ft (≈7.5 m)
Leaves
Large, leathery, evergreen; about 5–10 inches long
Flowers
Pink to rose-lavender clusters; late spring to early summer
Native range
British Columbia → Washington & Oregon → northern California (coast & Cascades)
Preferred climate
Cool, maritime/mesic climates; mild, wet winters
Habitats
Open conifer forests, clearcuts, roadcuts, and subalpine slopes with acidic soils

Where it Grows

Most common in western Washington and Oregon, extending north into British Columbia and south into northern California, from coastal forests up into the Cascade Mountains.

Climate & Conditions

  • Partial shade to dappled light (full sun is fine in cool coastal areas).
  • Acidic, well-drained soils with consistent moisture.
  • Protection from hot afternoon sun and drying winds improves vigor.
